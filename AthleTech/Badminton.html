Name
Single Court for Badminton
Description

Badminton is an exciting and dynamic racket sport played on a rectangular court divided by a high net. It can be played in singles (one player per side) or doubles (two players per side), and the objective is to score points by hitting a feathered shuttlecock over the net so that it lands in the opponent's court. The game starts with one player serving the shuttlecock from behind the service line, and it must be struck below the waist. Players use a variety of shots, including smashes, clears, drops, and drives, to outmaneuver their opponents. The fast pace and quick rallies require agility, speed, and strategic thinking, as players anticipate and react to their opponent's moves. Badminton is played to 21 points in most formats, with matches typically best of three games. A player or team must win by a two-point margin, adding an element of suspense and competition.
